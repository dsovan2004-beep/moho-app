## 1. Executive Summary

MoHoLocal is an AI-powered local community platform designed to become the digital operating system for fast-growing communities, starting with Mountain House, California, and expanding across nearby San Joaquin County cities such as Tracy, Lathrop, and Manteca.

The product combines the most durable parts of three proven local models:

- **Yelp** — local business discovery and search intent
- **Nextdoor** — neighborhood participation and community-generated content
- **Patch** — local information, updates, and civic relevance

The strategic opportunity is not to build a generic directory. The opportunity is to build AI-powered local infrastructure: a platform that helps residents discover businesses, share neighborhood knowledge, discover events, reunite lost pets, and eventually receive AI-generated local summaries and recommendations.

From an acquisition perspective, MoHoLocal should be designed as a repeatable city-platform system with strong local data, strong community participation, and a scalable operating model that does not require a large editorial or operations team.

## 3. Mission

Launch MoHoLocal in Mountain House, prove strong local engagement, refine the city-platform model, and expand across nearby cities using the same infrastructure.

The mission has three phases:

1. Establish utility
2. Activate contribution
3. Build defensible local data and revenue

---

## 4. Product Thesis

Local platforms historically fail for one of three reasons:

1. They are too static
2. They rely on too much manual editorial work
3. They do not create repeat usage

MoHoLocal addresses these problems by combining:

- structured local data
- user contribution loops
- AI assistance for moderation, summaries, and content generation

This creates a more efficient and scalable local platform than legacy directory or neighborhood products.

---

## 5. Why Mountain House First

Mountain House is a strong launch city because it has:

- rapid population growth
- high percentage of new residents
- strong Facebook-group behavior (high discovery intent)
- limited high-quality local digital infrastructure
- strong recommendation-driven discovery behavior
- a highly practical need for local services, schools, events, and neighborhood support

This makes Mountain House an ideal proving ground for a local platform that can later expand to Tracy, Lathrop, Manteca, Stockton, and other nearby markets.

## 6. Core Product Modules

### 6.1 Local Business Directory

**Purpose:** Capture high-intent search traffic and solve local recommendations.

Core functions: browse by city · browse by category · search by keyword · business detail pages · city badges and category filters · featured listings · claim listing workflow

### 6.2 Community Board

**Purpose:** Create habit, local participation, and neighbor-to-neighbor engagement.

Core functions: create post · filter by city · filter by topic · post details and replies · topic categories: general, recommendations, for sale, free items, jobs, services, safety, neighbors, questions

### 6.3 Events

**Purpose:** Make the platform part of weekly local life.

Core functions: upcoming events · past events · city-based relevance · venue and organizer details

### 6.4 Lost & Found Pets

**Purpose:** Create high-emotion engagement and sharing.

Core functions: lost / found / reunited tracking · city filter · pet details and contact info · photo upload

### 6.5 New Resident Guide

**Purpose:** Capture SEO traffic and serve fast-growing residential demand.

Core functions: utilities · schools · grocery and essentials · local tips · neighborhood orientation

### 6.6 Suggest a Business

**Purpose:** Crowdsource directory expansion.

### 6.7 Report Listing

**Purpose:** Maintain trust and data quality.

### 6.8 Claim This Business

**Purpose:** Create monetization and owner engagement.

## 7. Product Principles

Every future feature and design decision must align with these principles.

### 7.1 Local First

Every page should reinforce local context.

### 7.2 Mobile First

The product must work naturally on phones before desktop.

### 7.3 Simplicity Over Complexity

MoHoLocal is a local platform, not an enterprise SaaS product. Avoid microservices, complex pipelines, and over-engineering. Prefer simple maintainable solutions.

### 7.4 Trust Before Growth

Approved listings, clear reporting, and clean UX matter more than feature count.

### 7.5 Participation Drives Value

User contribution loops should increase over time.

### 7.6 AI as an Operating Multiplier

AI should reduce moderation cost, content cost, and data maintenance cost — not be the product headline for residents.

---

## 8. AI Strategy

AI is not the product headline for residents. AI is the hidden operating advantage.

### 8.1 AI Moderation

Use AI to detect: spam · scams · abusive content · repetitive junk posts

### 8.2 AI Summaries

Generate: weekly local summaries · featured business descriptions · event roundups · city-specific content

### 8.3 AI Local Search

Eventually support natural-language local queries such as: "best brunch in Tracy" · "family-friendly Mountain House" · "dog groomer near Lathrop open weekends"

### 8.4 AI Business Enrichment

AI can help draft: listing descriptions · category normalization · missing metadata suggestions

### 8.5 AI Community Infrastructure

Long term, MoHoLocal becomes AI-powered local infrastructure, not merely a static content site.

---

## 9. Design Standard for All Future Work

Before adding any feature, ask:

1. Does this improve local usefulness?
2. Does this improve repeat usage?
3. Does this improve trust?
4. Does this increase contribution?
5. Can this scale city to city?
6. Does this keep the platform simple?

**If the answer is no, do not build it yet.**
